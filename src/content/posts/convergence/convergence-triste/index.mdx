Triste is a young elf whose presence always seems to drift a half-step ahead of anyone trying to understand her. She moves like moonlight slipping through branches, soft in voice and impossible to pin down. Although she has been alone for as long as she can remember, and not a member of the various Elf families of this world, Triste carries that same quiet strangeness, as if she is listening to things no one else can hear.

---

A Seeker of Experience.

Curiosity rules her life. She seeks experience for its own sake, not gold or renown. Old ruins, whispered legends, forgotten relics, and reckless journeys appeal to her far more than comfort or safety. For reasons she has never explained, she often chooses to bring a companion along, tugging him into danger with nothing more than a smile and a spark in her eye.

That companion is Garreth Vale, a grizzled hunter and former soldier whose past has been carved into him by hard years and harder choices. Garreth is steady, practical, and slow to trust, but Triste disarmed him from the start. Somewhere along their shared travels, his loyalty grew into something deeper. He has not spoken of it, and she has never asked, but their closeness is evident in quiet glances and the way they move in step without thinking.

Whether she returns his feelings remains a mystery. Her affection is gentle and constant, but her inner thoughts stay carefully veiled.

--- 

Recently Triste learned of torrent of magical power that ripped a chasm deep into the earth. Something about it calls to her, singing her name as if beconing her home. Instead of revealing her true interest, she discovers that a traveling mage is also searching for the source of the magical outburst and is looking to hire protection for the journey. Triste convinces Garreth to take the job, offering nothing more than the promise of a curious path ahead. Garreth agrees because when Triste asks, he rarely says no.

Now the three of them walk beneath the tall boughs of the forest. The mage mutters over his notes, Garreth keeps a hunter’s watch, and Triste guides them with unobtrusive nudges whenever the forest seems to shift around them. She knows they are drawing closer to what she seeks.

---

Their path changes when they encounter two figures in a woodland clearing: a dwarf with the look of someone carrying old burdens, and beside him a small wanderer gazing upward at the sunlight with childlike wonder. Thorek and Rollo seem as surprised as anyone, though Triste feels a faint stir in the air, as though the world itself has begun stitching their stories together.

She steps forward with her usual serene smile, Garreth only a half-step behind her, ready for whatever comes next.
